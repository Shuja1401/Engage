Methodology

How Engage collects, cleans, and analyses YouTube data — and the choices behind every metric.

Channel universe

Engage tracks 21 English-language news channels across three segments. Segments are kept separate throughout all analysis: a television channel is never benchmarked against a digital-native outlet. The segmentation reflects fundamentally different production capacities, distribution histories, and audience expectations.

  • Television (7): Times Now, Republic TV, NDTV, CNN-News18, India Today TV, Mirror Now, NewsX
  • Print (7): Hindustan Times, The Telegraph, Times of India, Indian Express, The Tribune, The Hindu, New Indian Express
  • Web-only (7): The Wire, The Quint, The Print, NewsLaundry, Oneindia English, The News Minute, Scroll.in

Data collection

Videos are fetched via the YouTube Data API v3 using the uploads playlist method (channel ID prefix UC→UU). The fetch window runs from 30 days prior to the previous day at 12:00 IST. A 24-hour gestation period is applied: no video enters analysis until at least 24 hours after publication. This normalises the early-burst advantage that recently published videos would otherwise hold.

Snapshots of view, like, and comment counts are stored daily. Ratios are always re-derived at query time from summed raw components — per-video ratios are never averaged.

Content type classification

Every video is classified into one of five types based on duration. The order of the conditional chain is fixed:

live_or_premiereDuration = 0 secondsNo — excluded
shortDuration ≤ 60 secondsYes
broadcast_archiveDuration > 14,400 seconds (4 hrs)No — excluded
livestreamDuration > 3,600 seconds (1 hr)Yes
regularEverything else (61s – 3,600s)Yes

broadcast_archive and live_or_premiere are excluded from all performance charts. They represent bulk content dumps rather than discrete editorial decisions, and including them would distort engagement and volume metrics.

Engagement score

The engagement score measures audience response relative to reach. It weights likes more heavily than comments, because comment sections on Indian news channels contain a high proportion of noise (bot activity, repetitive political statements).

Engagement score = (Likes × 3.0 + Comments × 1.0) ÷ Views

The score is computed by summing raw components across all videos in a group first, then dividing — never by averaging per-video scores. This prevents low-view videos from distorting the channel-level figure.

Editorial effort

Editorial effort is a composite score that captures both the volume and total duration of videos published. It serves as a proxy for production investment. Channels are ranked within their own segment — a digital outlet's editorial effort score is not comparable to a television channel's.

Views per hour (VPH)

VPH normalises views against the age of the video at the time of snapshot capture. It answers a more honest question than raw views: how quickly is this video accumulating attention? It is the primary metric for identifying breakout content regardless of when in the month it was published.

Shelf life

Shelf life is a retrospective metric calculated monthly. It is defined as the number of days a video takes to accumulate 80% of its total 30-day view count. Only videos with more than 1,000 views and at least 30 days of snapshot data are included. A short shelf life indicates breaking-news content; a long shelf life suggests evergreen value.
